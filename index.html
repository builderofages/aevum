Fair questions, straight answers

What does term life actually cost?

It depends on your age, health, coverage amount, and term length — for many healthy adults in their 30s it's comparable to a couple of streaming subscriptions a month. The honest answer is a real quote, which takes about two minutes and costs nothing. One thing that's always true: rates are locked to the age you apply, so waiting has a price.

Do I need a medical exam?

Most healthy applicants qualify for no-exam (accelerated) underwriting and can get a decision quickly — sometimes the same day. Some situations still call for full underwriting; when that happens it usually means we're chasing you a better rate class, and we'll tell you why it's worth it.

I was declined by an online life insurance site. Is it over?

No — and this is genuinely our favorite kind of case. Instant-decision algorithms decline anything they can't neatly score. A human advisor with access to many carriers, including ones that specialize in specific health histories, can very often find coverage the algorithm couldn't.

I have coverage through work. Isn't that enough?

Employer coverage is a good start, but it's usually 1–2× salary and it typically ends when the job does. Most families that depend on an income need more, owned personally, that follows you between jobs. The free Legacy Audit will show you your exact gap — you may find work coverage is enough, and we'll tell you if so.

How does Aevum get paid?

Like every insurance agency, we're paid a commission by the carrier when a policy is placed — it's built into the premium and doesn't change your price versus buying direct. What's different here is that we're independent: our incentive is to place you with the right carrier, not the only carrier we represent.

Is Aevum an insurance company?

No — we're an independent licensed insurance agency. Your policy is issued by a third-party insurance carrier, and all guarantees are backed solely by the issuing carrier. Our job is to shop the market for you, advocate through underwriting, and stay your advisor after the sale.
